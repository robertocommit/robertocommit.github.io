HandsON
This page (currently under development) has the goal to illustrate what Roberto is able to achieve practically speaking. All these skills have been learn during previous work experiences, but also building personal projects or experiments.
BI Audit
The way an organisation implement and structure a Business Intelligence software is one of the first signals about potential inefficiencies. Often from inside an organisation, it is not possibile to understand opportunities and limitation, this is why having a professional looking at the tool from different angles it is extremely important.


BI Maintenance
A Business Intelligence software requires regular maintenance. From managing permissions to relocate queries, the number of daily hidden small tasks necessary to keep a system healty and up to date is often underestimate. Any organisation would require a specific role to take care of these aspects, but it is not always the case. Externalise this activity is a brave decision able soon pay to high dividends.


Query Optimisation
Nobody likes slow queries. Data needs to be deliver fast to enahnce decisions and move the business forward. Sometimes queries can be written better, while in other cases tables' structure can be optimised allowing faster results. Moreover, queries' style must be consistent, allowing a smart reuse of the code already written.


Crawling
Any company faces the need to extract data from the web. For a multitude of reasons, having the possibility to acquire data previously not available can lead to a competititive advantage. Data are out there, but often not easy to access, because of time constrains or technical limitations. Rely on reilable and scalable web crawers to bring your data to a next level.


Features Engineering
The biggest part of a machine learning project is the iterative process of features defining and features improvement. This activity can be considered boring from a data scientist point of view, because it is not directly connected with the algorithms behind the learning pipeline. However, this part is crucial and important as the algorithm itself if not even more. Get support and help not only to define features, but also produce and manintain them.


Automation
This section does not need presentation. A digital business not able to automise processes will face sooner or later big troubles. Growing a team instead of building tools is the perfect way to waste money and energies. Automate from the day one in order to keep focusing on what really matters: your business.


Marketing Automation
Marketing is probably the department where automation can be more significant and disruptive. Not a case, marketing is also one of the biggest cost cente. Specific skills and talent is needed to drive leads to a company, that's why time cannot be wasted dealing with data in unproductive ways. Free your marketing department from non value added activities leaving information's flow in expert hands.

KPIs Audit
KPIs are at the core of a business. Theoretically any process would require to be monitored by a KPI. There is however the bad habit to spend more time defining and building KPIs then using it. Let's make a step back, identify metrics that really matter and evolve the way they are produced and consumed.


Analysis
Diving into data with the goal to analyze them is a must have skill for any person working in the digital industry. However, often things get more complex and articulated. Specific tools and techniques are required to provide puntual, correct and reprudicible analysis.


Reporting
In the last ten years the activity of reporting has known an immense switch due to the rise of web technologies. Nowdays is not accattable anymore to rely on a person to receive a report at the same hour on a daily basis. Understand how to modernize your whole reporting structure.


Dashboard
A dashboard should not only be considered as data, queries and charts. A dashboard is a story, combining design, aesthetics, UX and UI. Details are essential, as colors, backgrounds, fonts, sizes, titles, positions, headers, alignments, approachability, efficiency and engagement. A dashboard is a real product with real use cases and real users.


API Development
API endpoint are critical for a business to allow external players to interact with internal data, but also to ensure a fluid flow of information within internal systems. Standardise the creation of API endpoints with modern and light technologies, while having always in control their cost and performances.


API Reverse Engineering
Activity strictly connected with web crawling, but also with computer security. Without dealing with espionage or unethical things, reverse engineering API is necessary because sometimes documentation of a service is not properly done, so there is the need to self understand how things work.


Scripting and tools
The necessity of a digital business are so variagate and colourful that are not possible to be categorised under a single umbrella. Any kind of small script and tool can save precious time, especially if an activity must be repeated multiple time. It is important to have a person in a team able to make colleagues' life easier and more pleasant.


Sheets Automation
For years many have tried to get rid of sheets, but the true is that they are here to stay. Another true is that sheets are getting better and better, making them easier to use, more intuitive and progrmamable. There are people with the talent to make sheets looking beauty and sexy, it is a luck to work closely with such a profile.


Notification
As anything else, also notifications have the right to be pleasant to be received and to be consumed. It is not time anymore to spam unreadable bad formatted messages, considering incredibile technologies available today for this purpose.


ETL
Extract, transform and load is the base of modern data engineering. Any company has the need to build reilable data pipelines, interating with API endpoints as well as databases. This activity if properly done allows a business to scale without perceving downtimes or inconsistencies.
